### üìã Activity Procedures & Teacher Guidance

#### **Activity 1: The Registrar's Data Entry (Interpretive)**
* **Procedure:** Teacher plays the role of a "School Registrar." Distribute a "Registration Form." Read 5 entries. For each, state the name once, then spell it slowly letter-by-letter.
* **The "Trap" Letters:** Include names with **H** (silent), **I** (sounds like English 'E'), **E** (sounds like English 'A'), and **G/J** (to test the 'h' sound).
* **Example:** *"Estudiante n√∫mero dos: Apellido, Qui√±ones. Se escribe: Q (cu) - U (u) - I (i) - √ë (e√±e) - O (o) - N (ene) - E (e) - S (ese)."*
* **Teacher Tip:** Provide a "Correction Round" where students check their neighbor's spelling against the board at the very end to provide immediate feedback.

#### **Activity 2: The Supply Closet (Interpersonal)**
* **Procedure:** Students work in pairs. 
    * **Student A (Clerk):** Has a visual "shopping list" of icons (e.g., 3 pencils, 2 folders).
    * **Student B (Manager):** Has a blank inventory sheet.
* **The Exchange:**
    * **A:** *"¬øMe das tres l√°pices, por favor?"*
    * **B:** *"¬øC√≥mo se escribe 'l√°piz'?"*
    * **A:** *"Ele - √° - pe - i - zeta."*
    * **B:** (Writes it down, then mimes handing it over) *"Aqu√≠ tienes."*
    * **A:** *"Muchas gracias."*
* **Teacher Tip:** Circulate with a "Checklist of 5." Try to hear 5 different pairs pronounce the letter **"I"** correctly as "eee."

#### **Activity 3: The Phonetic Desk Map (Presentational)**
* **Procedure:** Students draw their immediate desk/workspace. They must label 5 items using the formula: **"Un/Una [Object]."** Below the label, they must write out the *names* of the letters as if they were spelling it aloud.
* **Example:** * **Label:** *Una carpeta* * **Phonetics:** *u - ene - a | ce - a - ere - pe - e - te - a*
* **Teacher Tip:** This allows you to see if students are confusing the *sounds* of the letters with the *names* of the letters.

### üöÄ Differentiation & Enrichment

#### **Support: For Students Struggling to Meet Targets**
* **The "Cheat Sheet" Anchor:** Provide a desk-tent with the alphabet and "sounds-like" English hints (e.g., **E** = **A**y).
* **Scaffolded Forms:** For Task 1, provide the "Registrar Form" with the first and last letters of each name already filled in.
* **Partial Tasking:** In Task 2, allow these students to focus on just 3 items instead of 5, emphasizing quality of pronunciation over quantity of words.

#### **Enrichment: For Students Achieving/Exceeding Targets**
* **The "Email Extension":** Ask students to create a school email for the names in Task 1, spelling out the symbols (e.g., *punto* for dot, *arroba* for @).
* **Inventory Conflict:** In Task 2, give the "Manager" a card that says "Out of Stock" for one item. The "Clerk" must then ask for a different item on the fly.
* **The Extra Letter:** Require these students to label 8 items and include at least two words with "special characters" like **√ë** or words requiring an accent mark.
